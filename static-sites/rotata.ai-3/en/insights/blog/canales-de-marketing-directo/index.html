Direct Marketing Channels: Email, WhatsApp or Telephone?

Introduction Direct marketing continues to be an essential tool for companies that want to connect directly with their potential and current customers, choosing which direct marketing channels are the […]

Ignacio San Luis Castellano · Jan 8, 2025 · 3 min read

Introduction

Direct marketing continues to be an essential tool for companies that want to connect directly with their potential and current customers, choosing which direct marketing channels are appropriate is key. However, technological evolution and changes in consumer habits have given rise to a range of options: from emails to messaging platforms such as WhatsApp or traditional methods such as phone calls.

In the B2B context, the main objective of these techniques is usually to close qualified meetings with potential clients, making each channel a crucial means to advance the sales process. This article analyzes the efficiency of each channel and how they have evolved in recent years.

Emails: The Classic that is Reinvented

Efficiency:

Emails have been one of the most used tools in direct marketing due to their low cost and wide accessibility. With open rates ranging between 15% and 25% depending on the sector, emails are especially effective for informative content campaigns, promotions and newsletters.

Advantages:

  • Automation and personalization: Tools like Mailchimp or HubSpot allow you to send highly personalized messages.
  • Detailed measurement: Metrics such as open rates, clicks and conversions are easy to monitor.
  • Scalability: Ideal for reaching large audiences.

Challenges:

  • Overload: Users' inboxes are full of messages, making it difficult to stand out.
  • Spam filters: Email providers' algorithms can block messages.

Recent Evolution:

With the incorporation of artificial intelligence, emails can now be more predictive, suggesting products or services based on user behavior.

WhatsApp: The Power of Instant Messaging

Efficiency:

WhatsApp has emerged as a powerful channel, especially in markets where smartphone penetration is high. With open rates exceeding 90%, this channel is ideal for short messages, confirmations, and personalized updates.

Advantages:

  • High engagement: Messages on WhatsApp are usually read almost immediately.
  • Two-way interaction: Allows more fluid and direct communication with customers.
  • Versatile formats: Supports text, images, videos and documents.

Challenges:

  • Privacy restrictions: Regulations such as GDPR limit the use of this channel.
  • Scalability: Mass campaigns can be challenging without business tools like WhatsApp Business API.

Recent Evolution:

The integration of WhatsApp with CRM tools and chatbots has expanded its capabilities, making it possible to automate responses and improve the customer experience.

Phone Calls: The Personal Approach

Efficiency:

Phone calls remain an effective option for establishing a personal connection, particularly in B2B sales and high-value services. Although its conversion rate can be high in certain cases, the negative perception of telemarketing has also grown.

Advantages:

  • Direct interaction: Allows you to resolve questions and personalize the message in real time.
  • Emotional connection: A human voice generates trust.

Challenges:

  • High cost: Calls require more resources than other channels.
  • Customer resistance: Many people find calls intrusive and choose not to answer.

Recent Evolution:

The use of automatic dialing systems and training in “consultative sales” have improved the efficiency of this channel. In addition, calls are now complemented by other channels, such as email, to prepare the customer before communication.

General Comparison

Conclusion

When we talk about direct Marketing channels, there is no perfect channel; The choice depends on the type of client, the objective of the campaign and the resources available. Companies should consider an omnichannel strategy that combines the best of each option, adapting to user preferences and taking advantage of the strengths of each medium to maximize results.
