Discover how to double your lead generation with a credible ROI

Analysis on lead generation and ROI for the ICT sector based on the MarTech 2024 Observatory (Omnitel & BPS) and the Computing report. (ht tps://www.computing.es/inteligencia-artificial/observatorio-martech-2025-que-nos-depara-el-mundo-del-marketing-b2b/).

(Figures reproduced for comment and analysis purposes.)

Lead generation and ROI management are essential to the health of your business

If you are CEO, Director of Sales or Marketing or partner of an Information Technology (IT) services and consulting firm of between 50 and 250 professionals, you know this feeling well: the sales funnel tightens every quarter, costs rise and the board wants proof that the investment in Marketing is converted into income. The MarTech2024 Observatory listened to more than a hundred Spanish business-to-business (B2B) marketing managers and confirms what we already sensed: the absolute priority is to generate quality leads and the second, almost close, is to demonstrate a credible Return on Investment (ROI). In fact, 63% of those surveyed admit that the lack of well-qualified registrations is their first barrier, and 60% place the acquisition of new clients on the podium of immediate challenges.

The study offers a photograph that is as crude as it is useful, we are going to analyze how and why you should adjust your strategy right now.

Pain #1: Lead Generation That Doesn't Feed the Real Pipeline

Volume doesn't make the party

Participating companies already allocate between 25% and 50% of the budget to content marketing—declared by 29.2% of the panel—and another 43.3% adds between an extra 10% and 25% to inbound tactics. In other words: almost three out of every four euros will go to creating and promoting content. However, only 35% feel satisfied with the conversion of those efforts. The message is clear: production is not enough; Without precision targeting and a solid nutrition process, volume is devalued.

Rusty databases

That quality is the first obstacle is not surprising when we look at the following data: six out of ten marketers confess that their CRM is still powered by listings purchased or contacts captured more than two years ago. In a market where professional turnover is around 20% annually, these records age at a relentless pace. The result is doubly costly: bounces that increase the cost per paid lead—which the study places at around €300 for complex B2B accounts—and frustrated sales prospecting teams who see emails sent unopened.

The short-term bias

Pressed by immediate objectives, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) tech reduce brand visibility: 74.2% invest less than 10% in offline brand awareness and 55.8% do the same in reachable digital channels. The report warns that this imbalance makes the Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) more expensive in the medium term, because each lead that enters an oversaturated market does so without the lubricant of prior trust.

What works according to the data

Even with limited resources, some companies stand out. What do they do differently? Two clues supported by the Observatory figures:

  • High context content. Companies that publish verifiable use cases—with improvement or savings figures—generate 67% more leads than those that limit their blog to generic articles. You don't need an army of copywriters; All it takes is three very well-told stories that resolve the doubts of your Ideal Customer Profiles (ICPs).
  • Proximity events. 84% of those surveyed give executive breakfasts and “boutique” webinars the best cost/opportunity ratio. It is not about filling an auditorium: the value appears when you bring together twenty decision-makers and filter the discourse towards their specific pain.

Pain #2: an ROI that no one believes

Sales as judge and party

Asked about the metric that ultimately decides success, 72.5% point to billing or a closed pipeline. Up to here, logical. The problem comes when Marketing needs more than six months to demonstrate traction and, in the meantime, financial management asks for monthly adjustments. In fact, 49.2% report the return of campaigns in quarterly or even shorter cycles, knowing that the average sales cycle in IT services exceeds nine months.

The black box of attribution

Only half of professionals report systematically cross-checking volume and quality data—Marketing Qualified Lead (MQL), Sales Qualified Lead (SQL) and CAC—. Much less—barely 39%—track the social engagement that precedes the conversion. Thus, the budget is negotiated on perceptions and not on multi-touch models. It is not surprising that 34% of upcoming spending is reserved for reporting tools and dashboards: the industry recognizes that without clear evidence, Marketing becomes a cost center.

The paradox of the forgotten brand

The obsession with instant ROI has relegated brand building to a tiny budget drawer. The irony is that organizations that maintain at least a 15% stable investment in brand awareness report, after one year, an acquisition cost that is up to 22% lower than their tactical competitors. The report raises the warning: cutting off brand presence can be as self-destructive as stopping paying for web hosting.

Four levers to turn the numbers around and increase the generation of qualified leads

Rebuild your first database

Start with a radical audit: clean up expired records, enrich key contacts, and deploy progressive forms to capture fresh data. The Observatory points out that the companies that made this “reset” saw the bounce rate drop by 35% and doubled the response to prospecting emails in one semester.

Less frequent, more focused content

Publishing for the sake of publishing is waste. The report highlights that companies with a calendar of well-positioned “pillar pages” generated 50% more relevant organic traffic than those that launched individual articles. Translate that traffic into leads with specific calls to action: automate ebooks, demos or diagnostics that are tied to the story you just told.

Create one micro-event per month

You don't need fairs; you need high value conversation. The study data shows that a monthly cycle of closed sessions—in-person or virtual—constantly feeds the pipeline. Companies that apply it obtain opportunities with a 30% probability of closing, compared to 18% of leads captured in cold forms.

A metric to mark the north, a mixed committee

Define an indicator that everyone understands: pipeline generated per euro invested or MQL→SQL ratio. Then create a Marketing-Sales committee that reviews that number every four weeks. Organizations that implemented this ritual saw budget approval cycles reduced by 18% and gained agility to redirect campaigns that do not bear fruit.

Action plan to increase lead generation in 90 days

  • Day 0-30 – Data audit, selection of three client stories and definition of the northern metric.
  • Day 31-60 – Launch of the first “pillar page” and design of the pilot micro-event.
  • Day61-90 – Activation of predictive scoring and dashboard shared with Sales and Finance.

The Observatory shows that those who integrate these three milestones in a quarter improve the conversion of lead to opportunity by 25% and maintain visibility without increasing costs.

Conclusion

The MarTech2024 Observatory leaves no room for excuses: the numbers confirm that CEOs of IT SMEs that want to grow have to balance science and perseverance. Science to clean data, automate scoring and measure what matters; perseverance to sustain a brand narrative that avoids fighting with a knife every opportunity. With a healthy registration base, sharp content, closeness events and a shared metric, leads are no longer a chronic pain and ROI becomes a conversation as tangible as billing.

The next step? Choose the lever with the greatest gap in your company and execute it without delay: the market will not wait for your funnel to sort itself out. Turning intention into opportunity depends on your ability to act now, always measure, and adjust quickly. Go for it!
