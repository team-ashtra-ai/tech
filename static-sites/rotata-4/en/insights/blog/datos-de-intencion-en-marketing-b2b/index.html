Intent Data in B2B Marketing: ABM and Inbound Marketing

Ignacio San Luis Castellano · Jan 22, 2025 · 4 min read

In the digital era, B2B marketing and sales strategies have radically evolved, understanding what customers want, how they look for it and when they need it from a digital perspective has become essential. Paying attention to customer behavioral signals is an excellent strategy to position your B2B business above your competitors. Let's see how B2B Marketing Intent data can help us do this.

What is intent data?

Intent data is a set of online actions that indicate interest from a company or individual. These actions include actions such as: searching for certain keywords, visiting specific pages or consulting content related to certain products.

This data reveals who is searching, what they are searching for, and with what level of interest. Companies can use these signals to align their strategies and generate better sales results.

Intent data in Account-Based Marketing (ABM)

Account-Based Marketing is one of the most effective strategies for B2B marketing, and intent data is critical to its success. According to recent studies, companies that use intent data for their ABM strategies experience a 70% increase in the conversion rate of key prospects and a 50% improvement in sales cycle efficiency. Knowing which specific accounts are actively researching related products or services allows businesses to precisely tailor their marketing efforts, focusing on potential customers' real needs.

ABM example with intent data:

Let's consider a strategic consulting company that seeks to attract large corporations interested in optimizing their operational processes. Thanks to intent data, they identify that a multinational pharmaceutical company has been exploring “improvements in supply chain efficiency.” Armed with this information, the marketing team designs a personalized campaign that includes a success story in the pharmaceutical sector and organizes a private webinar for executives where specific solutions to optimize supply chains are addressed.

This approach demonstrates a deep understanding of the needs of the target company and positions the consultancy as the ideal partner to address its strategic challenges, significantly increasing the chances of conversion.

Intent data in Inbound Marketing

In B2B marketing, Inbound Marketing plays a crucial role in attracting customers to your brand through relevant content and unique experiences. Intent data helps you fine-tune these strategies by providing insights into how users interact with your content.

Inbound Marketing Example with Intent Data:

Suppose a company detects that several technology SMEs are looking for information about "cloud security strategies." In response, they created a series of blogs and an e-book titled “10 Best Practices for Protecting Data in the Cloud.” In addition, they organize a free webinar with a computer security expert.

This content not only attracts an interested audience, but also positions the company as an opinion leader in its sector. By analyzing what content is being consumed and by whom, they can offer personalized recommendations that increase the chances of conversion.

Intent data types

First-party intent data: Collected directly from your digital assets, such as:

  • The pages visited on your website
  • Pexplored products
  • Content downloaded or consulted.

Third-party intent data: Obtained from external sources, such as:

  • Reviews of similar products on other platforms.
  • Interactions in specialized forums.
  • Keywords researched in search engines.

Both types of data are essential: first-party data gives you direct information about your prospects, while third-party data helps you identify potential leads who have not yet interacted with you.

How to convert intent data into useful information for your business?

Collecting data is only the first step; The real challenge is converting that information into actions that generate results. Companies must develop clear strategies, based on business objectives, aligning data with internal processes and encouraging collaboration between sales and marketing. Let's look at three effective ways to use intent data:

Focus your commercial actions on real business opportunities: Integrating intent data with your CRM allows you to identify new active prospects and continually update them. In addition to identifying the most promising leads, you can prioritize those with the highest probability of conversion by analyzing their interaction patterns. This ensures that your contact list is not only accurate and relevant, but also strategic.

Define winning marketing strategies: Leveraging intent data allows you to identify the specific preferences of your leads and design personalized strategies to maximize their conversion. For example, if a prospect interacts multiple times with a report on market trends, you can send them a personalized invitation to an exclusive roundtable with industry experts or an interactive workshop related to those trends. This level of personalization not only captures their interest, but also positions your company as a valuable partner in strategic decision making.

Detect upselling and cross-selling opportunities: Analyzing existing customer interactions can reveal interest in new products or services. If a current customer shows interest in content about an advanced functionality, you can organize a one-on-one consulting session to show how that functionality can be integrated into their current operations and optimize their results.
