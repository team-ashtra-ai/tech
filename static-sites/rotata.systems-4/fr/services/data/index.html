Data matters when it changes who gets action first

Good data is not a database size story. It is a decision quality story.

Review the system See related solution
01

Contexte

Role in the system

Data sets priority. It decides which accounts enter the motion, which contacts matter and what the team can trust before acting.

02

Modèle opératoire

Data sources

Useful data comes from a small number of sources that can be trusted, matched and reviewed.

CRM records and form inputs
Enrichment providers and firmographic data
Buying-role and account hierarchy data
Engagement and intent signals
03

Couche système

Enrichment logic

Enrichment should close the gaps that block routing, segmentation and account coverage.

Normalize core fields

Keep company, contact and lifecycle data usable across workflows.

Fill missing context

Add the fields needed for segmentation, ownership and prioritization.

Validate freshness

Remove stale records before they distort decisions.

Map account coverage

Link buying roles and account relationships to real commercial use.

04

Processus

Signal processing

Signals only matter when they are validated, scored and sent into the workflow at the right moment.

01

Collect the event or enrichment input

02

Validate relevance and recency

03

Score fit, timing or account priority

04

Sync the result into CRM and queues

05

Modèle opératoire

Prioritization

Priority should be based on fit, timing and account context rather than opinion or recency bias.

High-fit accounts move up the queue
Buying-role gaps become visible
Stale records fall out of active coverage
Teams spend less time researching before action
06

Preuve

Outputs

The output should be visible in live commercial use.

Cleaner segmentation
Better target account prioritization
Less manual research
More confidence in next-step choice
