Introduction: a silent (but radical) paradigm shift

In recent months, Google has begun to show a completely new version of its search engine. It has not done so with major advertising campaigns or logo changes, but the impact of this transformation could redefine the rules of the game for all companies that depend on online visibility. This new experience, known as Search Generative Experience (SGE), uses advanced language models to provide complete answers to users' questions without requiring them to click on any links. The information appears directly generated by AI, at the top of the search engine, displacing traditional results.

This marks the birth of a new paradigm: from SEO (Search Engine Optimization) to IAO (Intelligent Answer Optimization).

And although, as we will see later, this change still does not affect all sectors equally, all companies and, as far as I am concerned, those of IT Services & ConsultingWe should anticipate: the search experience is evolving in our sector, where purchasing decisions are complex, the commercial cycle is long and trust in the brand is key. Is your company prepared to compete in an environment where the click will no longer be the main conversion vehicle?

The rules of positioning are changing (and no one has told you about it)

Until now, SEO was based on optimizing titles, links, keywords and content structure to appear among the first results on Google. That's still important, but it's no longer enough.

With the arrival of the IAO, the rules are rewritten:

  • Your content should be cited by AI, not just indexed by Google.
  • The authority of your brand gains weight, because AI prioritizes “trustworthy and relevant” sources.
  • Context matters more than keywords: AI looks for semantic patterns, consistency and intent.
  • Alternative formats (podcasts, videos, structured data, user opinions) take center stage.

In other words: your brand no longer competes just to appear; compete to be credible in the eyes of a machine.

How will Google Generative Search (SGE) affect B2B searches in 2025?

Over the last few years, many analysts anticipated a dramatic drop in organic traffic due to the rise of “zero-click traffic”. However, recent reports suggest that these predictions may have been exaggerated. In fact, according to an article published in PuroMarketing in January 2025, the reality is that zero-click is not occurring in the radical way that was feared (PuroMarketing, 2025). Google's AI has not eliminated the click, but is changing how and where it occurs.

In summary: we are not facing the end of organic traffic, but we are facing a deep transformation in visibility patterns. Clicks have not disappeared, but they are being redistributed according to the value, authority and structure of the content we offer.

For a company like yours, this has direct consequences:

  • Your website may appear as a source of information... but the user will never visit it if the summary already resolves their question.
  • Your content can feed Google's AI... but without explicitly mentioning you.
  • Your positioning strategy can be impeccable... and still not generate opportunities if it does not adapt to the new search format.

The new search environment: How does Google answer your customers' questions?

Google's behavior regarding the use of the SGE obeys criteria that depend on many factors. To illustrate this, we have done a small experiment: let's assume that a client wants to search for CRM (Customer Relationship Management) software for his company and asks different queries on Google.

In a first attempt we have put the following query in English: “crm software for small companies” and the result has been an activation of SGE (see Fig. 1).

![SERP in English with active SGE showing list of CRMs](sandbox:/mnt/data/Captura 1.PNG) Fig. 1 — Query in English (“crm software for small companies”): Active SGE with list of tools. Screenshot taken in August 2025 (Spain, desktop, session not logged in).

However, when we have changed the phrase for another very similar one, but in Spanish —“I am looking for customer management software for small businesses”—, the result has been a more traditional response, without activation of the SGE, where the sponsored content appears at the beginning of the results page (SERP) (see Fig. 2).

![SERP in Spanish without SGE with featured ad](sandbox:/mnt/data/Captura 2.PNG) Fig. 2 — Specific query in Spanish (“I am looking for customer management software for small businesses”): without SGE; priority to ads. Screenshot taken in August 2025 (Spain, desktop, session not logged in).

We have repeated the query about CRM software combining Spanish and English using similar phrases and we have drawn several conclusions:

Conclusions of the experiment

  • Queries in English almost always push the SGE to the top; sometimes as the first option, other times right after the sponsored content.
  • Queries in Spanish are most often answered in the traditional way (without SGE), but as the phrase is more generic, a response that includes SGE is more likely.
  • If we use a more generic phrase in Spanish, it is more likely to activate the SGE. The query “CRM Solutions” did activate the SGE, as we see in Fig. 3, probably because Google interprets that you may be looking for information about what a “CRM” is.

![SERP in Spanish with active SGE defining CRM and functions](sandbox:/mnt/data/Captura 4.PNG) Fig. 3 — Generic Spanish query (“CRM Solutions”): Active SGE with definition and functions. Screenshot taken in August 2025 (Spain, desktop, session not logged in).

As if that were not enough, Search Engine Journal highlights that SGE coverage varies significantly depending on the sector and that, in the case of financial searches, for example, it only reaches 47%, compared to more than 90% in sectors such as beauty, automotive or eCommerce (Search Engine Journal, 2024). See Fig. 4.

At this point, I invite you to do your own tests trying to simulate those phrases for which you think your potential clients should search for you on Google.

What is clear is that, although the SGE does not yet affect all verticals equally and depends on many factors for its activation, everything indicates that Google will progressively expand its coverage, and brands must prepare for that scenario.

Therefore, if your content is not optimized to be understood and cited by AI, you could lose visibility just when your sector begins to be impacted.

No clicks. No forms. Without giving you the opportunity to capture that lead.

What it means for your B2B company and what to do now

Your sector is already in SGE's sights: the uncertain thing is not if it affects you, but how much it is affecting you today in terms of visibility and conversion. That is why it is advisable to act urgently. The priority is that your brand is citable by Google AI and that your authority signals and data are ready to be interpreted by intelligent systems.

At Rotata we help companies like yours respond to this change with concrete actions. This is the unified, actionable and priority plan:

  1. Diagnose your visibility and authority.
    1. Check mentions and backlinks (incoming links from other websites) and citations in media/portals of the sector.
    1. Check if your content is referenced by third parties and if you appear as a source.
  2. Refocus the content towards authority and response.
    1. Start with a direct answer and follow with explanation, lists and FAQs (frequently asked questions) easy to cite by AI.
    1. Use basic structured data and clear formats (tables, videos, own studies).
  3. Prepare your data and fundamentals.
    1. Integrate CRM and analytics; maintain consistent product/service names and clean data.
    1. Ensures that key information is accessible and consistent across all touchpoints.
  4. Activate AI and measure impact with simple metrics.
    1. Use tools like ChatGPT, DataRobot or Breeze to optimize content and detect intent, and train the team to use them judiciously.
    1. Follow visibility (SGE appearances/citations) and business (MQL, Marketing Qualified Leads, and funnel velocity) goals and adjust.
  5. Prioritize key accounts (ABM, Account-Based Marketing) and personalization.
    1. Define target companies and messages by profile; automates multichannel prospecting.
    1. Orchestra campaigns that respond to intent signals, not just keywords.

Act now: The sooner you measure and adjust, the less dependence you will have on sudden changes in the generative results page (SERP) (SGE).

Conclusion: from passive spectator to protagonist of your digital visibility

It is likely that, as someone responsible for generating business, you ask yourself: “And is this going to help us sell more?” The answer is yes, visibility is no longer a matter of being there, but of being chosen by intelligent algorithms that prioritize relevance, consistency and experience.

AI is not replacing SEO; It is transforming it into somethingmore complex and challenging. But also fairer: it is no longer the one who produces the most content that wins, but the one who provides the most value to the user.

Adapt quickly makes the difference between leading or disappearing from the radar. Your sector is already in the crosshairs of generative AI; What is still unclear is to what extent SGE is affecting your visibility and conversion today. Precisely because of this uncertainty it is advisable to measure, experiment and adjust now, before the impact is fully visible and the rules of the game change completely.

You can wait for Google to rewrite the conditions of digital visibility, or you can anticipate. Preparing your company for this transition is not just an advantage: it is a strategic necessity.
