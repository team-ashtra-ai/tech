HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report: Key Trends in B2B Marketing

Ignacio San Luis Castellano · Feb 2, 2026 · 4 min read

The HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report analyzes how artificial intelligence, automation and results orientation are redefining B2B marketing.

In a context of greater pressure on ROI and more demanding budgets, the report confirms a clear trend: marketing is definitively entering an AI-driven stage, where efficiency, data quality and alignment with sales are critical success factors.

In this article we summarize and analyze the main conclusions of HubSpot's State of Marketing 2026, with a practical approach aimed at marketing, sales and operations managers who need real impact on business, not just activity.

Artificial Intelligence in B2B marketing according to the HubSpot 2026 Report

One of the strongest messages from the HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report is that artificial intelligence is no longer experimental. In 2026, AI will become part of the basic infrastructure of modern marketing.

The best performing companies already use AI systematically to:

  • Prioritize leads and accounts with the highest probability of conversion
  • Automate low-value operational tasks
  • Personalize messages and campaigns at scale
  • Analyze performance and attribute ROI more accurately

The real differential is not in “using AI”, but in integrating it into real business processes, connecting marketing, sales and data in a single operational flow.

Demand generation and quality of leads in the State of Marketing 2026

The report confirms a profound change in B2B marketing priorities: the end of the approach based on lead volume.

The most advanced organizations are abandoning massive strategies to focus on:

  • Quality vs. quantity
  • Segmentation based on real intent
  • Predictive lead scoring models
  • Account-Based Marketing (ABM) Strategies

This change responds to a recurring problem: bloated databases, saturated teams and unreliable pipelines. AI allows you to filter earlier, prioritize better and concentrate resources on opportunities with a real probability of closing.

Marketing and sales alignment: one of the great challenges of B2B marketing in 2026

Despite technological advancement, the HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report confirms that the misalignment between marketing and sales continues to be one of the main brakes on growth.

The best performing companies share three clear characteristics:

  1. A CRM as a single data core
  2. Shared definitions of MQL, SQL and opportunity
  3. Common dashboards geared toward revenue, not just activity

Technology is no longer the problem. The challenge is to design shared processes, where marketing and sales work with the same data, objectives and success metrics.

Smart automation: efficiency without losing control

The report shows that automation continues to grow, but with an important nuance: automating without a strategy no longer works.

Leading companies use automation to:

  • Reduce repetitive manual tasks
  • Speed up response times
  • Ensure consistent tracking
  • Scaling without increasing structure

The difference in 2026 is that automation increasingly relies on contextual AI, capable of adapting to real user behavior and not just static rules.

ROI, analytics and performance measurement in B2B marketing 2026

In a more demanding economic context, HubSpot's State of Marketing 2026 places ROI at the center of the strategy.

The marketing teams with the best results are strengthening:

  • More accurate attribution models
  • Reporting directly connected to revenue
  • Metrics aimed at efficiency and conversion
  • Full funnel visibility in real time

Marketing is no longer justified by volume of actions and is now measured by direct contribution to business growth.

What the HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report means for B2B companies

The message of the report is clear: it is not about adding more tools, but rather about building an integrated, intelligent and results-oriented system.

For most B2B companies, the challenge is not technological, but strategic:

  • Integrate AI without friction
  • Reduce the complexity of the technological stack
  • Align teams and processes
  • Prioritize real opportunities
  • Climb without losing control or visibility

Conclusion: AI-First marketing consolidates in 2026

The HubSpot State of Marketing 2026 Report confirms an already unstoppable trend: B2B marketing is evolving towards a more intelligent, automated and revenue-oriented model.

Organizations that adopt an AI-First approach, integrate technology with processes and align marketing and sales will be in a clearly superior position to compete in the coming years.
