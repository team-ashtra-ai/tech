HubSpot Report on the State of Marketing in 2025

Ignacio San Luis Castellano · Apr 10, 2025 · 5 min read

This 2025 is presented as a key year for marketing, and this is clearly reflected in the HubSpot 2025 Report, especially with regard to the incorporation of Artificial Intelligence. If you're not already integrating AI into your strategy, you're likely missing out on an important competitive advantage. And I'm not saying this to jump on a fad, but because the data speaks for itself: 92% of marketing professionals acknowledge that AI has already transformed the way they work. This is not a temporary trend, but a new foundation on which current marketing is built.

In this article I share the main conclusions of the HubSpot report on the state of marketing in 2025. A profound transformation is coming, but multiple opportunities also open up for you, if you know how to adapt strategically.

AI as a marketing engine

One of the most notable transformations is that Artificial Intelligence has stopped being a complement and has become the central axis of modern marketing. We are no longer just talking about automating campaigns, but about helping you reinvent the way you conceive, produce and optimize your content.

HubSpot introduces the concept of «multimodal campaigns«, which allows you to transform an idea into multiple formats: text, video, audio, pieces for social networks, etc., all from a single content source. This allows you to gain speed, efficiency and scalability.

In addition, the use of AI agents such as «Breeze Copilot» facilitates the planning, execution and analysis of campaigns, acting as intelligent assistants that accompany your marketing team at every stage of the process.

From traffic to attention: the new KPI

The focus is no longer just on generating traffic, but on capturing and maintaining attention. This requires a change in focus: creating more authentic, relatable content with a solid narrative. The brands that work best are those that express themselves with their own voice and connect from a human point of view.

It is increasingly common to see content led by real people—such as managers or employees—who provide closeness and trust. From informal videos to mini podcasts, marketing is humanized, and this generates a tangible impact.

Brands with purpose, not just with product

The report highlights that the brands that are standing out in 2025 are those that communicate their values ​​clearly: sustainability, diversity, social impact... and they do so honestly, not as just another campaign.

65% of marketing professionals claim that talking about social issues has helped them connect better with their audiences. In a saturated environment, differentiating yourself by purpose is more effective than competing on price alone.

Own data and privacy: the new standard

With the disappearance of third-party cookies and greater regulation on privacy, first-party data takes on absolute importance. If you want to remain competitive, you will have to invest in knowing your audience directly.

This means having the right infrastructure to ethically collect, process and activate your own data. And also work on the quality of that data: purify it, enrich it and keep it updated.

HubSpot, for example, has launched features to automate these types of tasks, allowing you to ensure that AI algorithms work with accurate information.

Personalization at scale, thanks to AI

Customization is no longer an option: it is an imperative. Campaigns that adapt to the user's behavior, language, interests and moment have significantly higher conversion rates.

Thanks to AI, today you can launch campaigns with dozens or hundreds of personalized variants efficiently. From dynamic emails and landing pages to localized content and specific recommendations. All, without having to scale your team.

Social networks: building community, not just presence

YouTube, Instagram and TikTok remain the most relevant channels, but the focus changes: it is no longer about volume, but about meaningful interaction. The ability to create community, encourage conversation and build trust is valued.

Marketing teams are incorporating more strategic and creative profiles, prioritizing genuine content over purely advertising content. Short video is consolidated as the format with the highest return on investment.

The rise of podcasting as a trusted channel

The podcast has become one of the most effective formats for generating authority and emotional connection. 91% of marketers plan to maintain or increase their investment in this channel.

Its value lies in prolonged attention: while a web visit lasts seconds, a listener can spend 30 minutes with you. It is a unique opportunity for you to tell your story, explain your value proposition and build loyal audiences.

The new search: AI and language models

Search habits are changing radically. New generations already use tools like ChatGPT, Claude or Perplexity to inform themselves and discover brands.

This requires adapting traditional SEO to a new approach: SEO for language engines. HubSpot has launched tools like AI Search Grader that allow you to measure the visibility of your brand in these environments.

Preparing your content to be referenced by these models will be key to maintaining your relevance.

AI + humanity: the essential balance

Technology alone is not enough. The true competitive advantage lies in how you combine the power of AI with the best of human talent: creativity, empathy and strategic vision.

AI frees up your time, eliminates repetitive tasks and enhances your capabilities. But the real value is still in authentically connecting with people.

Therefore, if there is one idea that I would like to convey to you for 2025, it is this:

It's not about replacing the human with artificial intelligence, but rather using AI to amplify what makes you unique.

In this new scenario, standing out will not only depend on the resources you have, but on how you use them to generate trust and real value.
