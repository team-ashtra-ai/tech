Solutions

Alignment happens when teams read the same system

Marketing and sales align when the system gives them one stage language, one handoff model and one feedback loop.

Review the system See related service
01

Friction

Misalignment problem

Alignment breaks when marketing and sales use different definitions, owners and success measures.

MQL and SQL mean different things in different rooms
Handoffs depend on personal interpretation
Sales feedback does not return in a usable format
Activity reporting hides quality disagreement
02

Modèle opératoire

Sales vs marketing gap

The gap shows up in duplicated data, disputed lead quality and reporting that hides disagreement instead of resolving it.

Different success measures drive different behaviour
Data is duplicated across teams
Rejection reasons are not reviewed cleanly
Escalation paths are unclear
03

Couche système

System alignment

Rotata aligns teams by giving both sides one qualification language, one routing model and one reporting reality.

Shared definitions

Qualification and stage logic mean the same thing everywhere.

Shared routing rules

Leads and opportunities move through agreed ownership logic.

Shared reporting views

Both teams read the same funnel and pipeline reality.

Shared review cadence

Feedback loops are scheduled into the system.

04

Processus

Workflow integration

The workflow should carry context from demand creation through handoff, acceptance and pipeline movement.

01

Capture demand and qualify it

02

Hand off with the right context

03

Record acceptance or rejection

04

Feed the learning back into targeting and messaging

05

Modèle opératoire

Feedback loops

Feedback loops keep the shared model honest once live work starts.

Accepted vs rejected leads by rule
Loss reasons returning to targeting
Speed and SLA visibility across teams
Weekly review actions tied to shared data
06

Preuve

Outcomes

When alignment holds, planning gets easier because teams are finally reading the same system.

Less debate about lead quality
A tighter connection between demand and sales
Clearer accountability across teams
Stronger revenue reporting

Align marketing and sales around one model
